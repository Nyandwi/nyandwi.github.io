About Me

I am Jean de Dieu Nyandwi. I work on machine learning and multimodal AI, and currently a visiting researcher at CMU LTI, where I am fortune to work with Prof. Graham Neubig and other amazing people in Neulab and CMU.


I recently completed MS Engineering AI at Carnegie Mellon University. I did undergrad in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering at University of Rwanda, learning machine learning on the side. Prior to that, I achieved the top score, country wide, in Advanced Level National Examinations in Electronics and Telecommunication, senior highschool.

Beside research, I also have interests in AI education and accessibility, and I spend a fair amount of time designing, writing deep dives, exploring, and sharing learning resources.

Outside technical works, I regularly workout, mostly resilience training, crossfit, calisthenics, basketball, and running.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in machine learning, natural language processing, and multimodal AI. More specifically, I work on improving and evaluating vision language models. I am currently exploring multimodal post-training, visual reasoning in VLMs, and data-efficient learning.

I have previously worked on adversarial evaluation in VLMs(NeurIPS 2024), grounding world knowledge in multimodal LLMs(EMNLP 2025), and open-source multilingual/cultural multimodal LLMs(Pangea ICLR 2024, CulturalPangea EMNLP 2025).

  if (typeof authors === 'string') return authors;
  if (Array.isArray(authors)) return authors.join(", ");
  return "";
